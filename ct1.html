Madras High Court

The Madras High Court is one of the high courts in India and is located in Chennai, the capital city of the southern state of Tamil Nadu.
Here are some details about the Madras High Court:



Establishment:

The Madras High Court was established on August 15, 1862, under the Indian High Courts Act, 1861.



Jurisdiction:

The jurisdiction of the Madras High Court extends over the state of Tamil Nadu and the Union Territory of Puducherry (formerly known as Pondicherry).



Bench:



Madurai Bench of Madras High Court



The Madras High Court has a principal seat in Chennai and has additional benches in Madurai and Madras (formerly known as Chennai) High Court Bench at Madurai

The Madras High Court Bench at Madurai was inaugurated on Saturday, the 24th July of 2004



Authority:



The Madras High Court has both original jurisdiction and appellate jurisdiction.
It handles both civil and criminal cases and has the power of judicial review to ensure the constitutionality of laws and government actions.



Chief Justice:

The Madras High Court is headed by the Chief Justice, who is appointed by the President of India in consultation with the Chief Justice of India.



Court Complex:



The Madras High Court is housed in a magnificent building known as the Madras High Court Building.
It is an architectural marvel and a prominent landmark in Chennai.



Case Types:

The Madras High Court deals with a wide range of cases, including civil disputes, criminal cases, writ petitions, appeals, and public interest litigations (PILs).

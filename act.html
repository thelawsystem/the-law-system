CRIMINAL LAW ACTS



Major criminal law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. Indian Penal Code (IPC) - 1860

    The IPC is the primary criminal law act in India.
    It was enacted in 1860 and defines various criminal offenses and their punishments.



  2. Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) - 1973

    The CrPC lays down the procedure for the investigation, trial, and punishment of criminal offenses.



  3. Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act (NDPS Act) - 1985

    This act regulates the production, sale, and consumption of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances in India.



  4. Prevention of Corruption Act (PCA) - 1988

    The PCA aims to prevent corruption and punish public officials who engage in corrupt practices.



  5. Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act (POCSO Act) - 2012

    This act provides protection to children from sexual offenses and lays down stringent punishment for offenders.



  6. Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act - 2000

    This act deals with juvenile offenders and provides for their care, protection, and rehabilitation.



  7. Indian Evidence Act - 1872

    This act lays down the rules of evidence in Indian courts.



  8. Dowry Prohibition Act - 1961

    This act prohibits the giving or taking of dowry in marriages.



  9. Information Technology Act - 2000

    This act deals with cyber crimes and provides for their punishment.



  10. The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 - 2013

    This act provides for the prevention, prohibition, and redressal of sexual harassment of women at the workplace.



CIVIL LAW ACTS



Some of the major civil law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. Indian Contract Act - 1872

    This act deals with contracts between individuals and lays down the rules for the formation and enforcement of contracts.



  2. Transfer of Property Act - 1882

    This act governs the transfer of property from one person to another, including sale, mortgage, lease, and gift.



  3. Sale of Goods Act - 1930

    This act regulates the sale of goods and lays down the rules for the transfer of ownership of goods from seller to buyer.



  4. Limitation Act - 1963

    This act lays down the rules for the period of limitation for filing civil suits, i.e., the time limit within which a legal action can be taken.



  5. Hindu Marriage Act - 1955

    This act governs the marriage and divorce of Hindus, including Sikhs, Jains, and Buddhists.



  6. Hindu Succession Act - 1956

    This act deals with the inheritance and succession of property among Hindus.



  7. Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act - 1937

    This act provides for the application of Muslim personal law in matters related to marriage, divorce, maintenance, and inheritance.



  8. Special Marriage Act - 1954

    This act provides for the marriage of people belonging to different religions and casts.



  9. Indian Divorce Act - 1869

    This act provides for the dissolution of marriage among Christians and lays down the rules for alimony, custody, and maintenance.



  10. The Foreign Marriage Act - 1969

    This act provides for the registration of marriages of Indian nationals solemnized outside India.





PROPERTY LAW ACTS



Some of the major civil law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. Transfer of Property Act, 1882:

    This law regulates the transfer of property in India.



  2. Registration Act, 1908:

    This law deals with the registration of documents, including property transactions.



  3. Indian Easements Act, 1882:

    This law governs the creation and termination of easements and rights of way.



  4. Land Acquisition Act, 1894:

    This act provides for the acquisition of land by the government for public purposes, and lays down the rules for compensation to be paid to the landowners.



  5. Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act - 1988

    This act prohibits the practice of holding property in the name of another person, i.e., benami transactions.



  6. Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act - 2016

    This act provides for the regulation and development of the real estate sector, including the establishment of a Real Estate Regulatory Authority to oversee the sector.



  7. The Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act - 2013

    This act provides for fair compensation, rehabilitation, and resettlement of landowners and others affected by land acquisition.





CONTRACT LAW ACTS



Some of the major Contract law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. Sale of Goods Act - 1930

    This act governs contracts for the sale of goods and lays down the rules for the transfer of ownership of goods from the seller to the buyer.



  2. Partnership Act - 1932

    This act provides the legal framework for partnerships, including the formation, rights, and obligations of partners, and the dissolution of partnerships.



  3. Specific Relief Act - 1963

    This act provides for the enforcement of certain special rights and obligations, such as specific performance of contracts, injunctions, and declarations.



  4. Consumer Protection Act - 1986

    This act provides protection to consumers against unfair trade practices and lays down the rules for consumer contracts.



  5. Foreign Contracts (Recognition and Enforcement) Act - 1979

    This act provides for the recognition and enforcement of foreign contracts in India.



  6. Information Technology Act - 2000

    This act deals with electronic contracts and transactions, including the formation, execution, and enforceability of electronic contracts.



  7. Arbitration and Conciliation Act - 1996

    This act provides for the resolution of disputes through arbitration, and lays down the rules and procedures for arbitration proceedings.

  8. The Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act - 2007

EMPLOYMENT LAW ACTS



Some of the major Employment law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act - 1946

    This act requires employers to define the conditions of employment in writing, and lays down the rules and procedures for framing and certifying standing orders.



  2. Payment of Wages Act - 1936

    This act provides for the payment of wages to employees, and lays down the rules and procedures for deductions from wages.



  3. Minimum Wages Act - 1948

    This act provides for the fixation of minimum wages for employees in certain industries.



  4. Employees' State Insurance Act - 1948

    This act provides for social security benefits to employees in the event of sickness, maternity, and employment injury.



  5. Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act - 1952

    This act provides for the establishment of a provident fund for employees and lays down the rules and procedures for its management.



  6. Factories Act - 1948

    This act provides for the health, safety, and welfare of workers employed in factories.



  7. Trade Unions Act - 1926

    This act provides for the registration and regulation of trade unions.



  8. Industrial Disputes Act - 1947

    This act provides for the settlement of industrial disputes between employers and employees, and lays down the rules and procedures for conciliation, arbitration, and adjudication.



  9. Maternity Benefit Act - 1961

    This act provides for the grant of maternity leave and maternity benefit to female employees.



  10. Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act - 2013

    This act provides for the prevention and redressal of sexual harassment of women at the workplace.



  11. Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act - 1986

    This act prohibits the employment of children in certain occupations and lays down the rules and procedures for regulating the working conditions of children.



  12. Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act - 1970

    This act regulates the employment of contract labour, and lays down the rules and procedures for its abolition in certain circumstances.



  13. Payment of Bonus Act - 1965

    This act provides for the payment of bonus to employees.





INTELLECTUAL LAW ACTS



Some of the major Intellectual law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. The Patents Act, 1970

    This act provides for the grant of patents for inventions, and lays down the rules and procedures for their registration, licensing, and enforcement.



  2. The Trade Marks Act, 1999

    This act provides for the registration and protection of trade marks, and lays down the rules and procedures for their enforcement.



  3. The Copyright Act, 1957

    This act provides for the protection of original literary, dramatic, musical, and artistic works, and lays down the rules and procedures for their registration, licensing, and enforcement.



  4. The Designs Act, 2000

    This act provides for the registration and protection of designs, and lays down the rules and procedures for their enforcement.



  5. The Geographical Indications of Goods (Registration and Protection) Act, 1999

    This act provides for the registration and protection of geographical indications, and lays down the rules and procedures for their enforcement.



  6. The Semiconductor Integrated Circuits Layout-Design Act, 2000

    This act provides for the registration and protection of layout-designs of integrated circuits, and lays down the rules and procedures for their enforcement.



  7. The Protection of Plant Varieties and Farmers' Rights Act, 2001

    This act provides for the protection of plant varieties and the rights of farmers, and lays down the rules and procedures for their enforcement.



  8. The Information Technology Act, 2000

    This act provides for the regulation of electronic transactions and data protection, and lays down the rules and procedures for their enforcement. It also includes provisions for the protection of intellectual property in the digital environment.





CONSTITUTIONAL LAW ACTS



Some of the major Constitutional law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. The Representation of the People Act, 1950

    This act provides for the preparation and revision of electoral rolls, the conduct of elections, and the qualifications and disqualifications for membership of parliament and the state legislatures.



  2. The Representation of the People Act, 1951

    This act provides for the conduct of elections to the office of the President of India and to fill vacancies in the office of the Vice President of India.



  3. The Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950

    This order lists the castes and communities that are deemed to be scheduled castes and are entitled to special protection under the Constitution of India.



  4. The Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950

    This order lists the tribes and communities that are deemed to be scheduled tribes and are entitled to special protection under the Constitution of India.



  5. The Constitution (Jammu and Kashmir) Order, 1954

    This order extends the provisions of the Constitution of India to the state of Jammu and Kashmir and sets out the terms of the relationship between the state and the Union of India.



  6. The Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Order, 1954

    This order provides for the application of the provisions of the Constitution of India to the state of Jammu and Kashmir and specifies the extent to which these provisions apply to the state.



  7. The Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order (Amendment) Act, 1976

    This act amends the Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950, to include new castes and communities in the list of scheduled castes.



  8. The Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order (Amendment) Act, 1976

    This act amends the Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950, to include new tribes and communities in the list of scheduled tribes.



  9. The National Commission for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Act, 1990

    This act establishes a national commission for the protection of the rights of scheduled castes and tribes and sets out its functions and powers.



  10. The National Commission for Backward Classes Act, 1993

    This act establishes a national commission for the protection of the rights of backward classes and sets out its functions and powers.





ADMINISTRATIVE LAW ACTS



Some of the major Administative law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. The Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985

    This act provides for the establishment of administrative tribunals to adjudicate disputes and complaints with respect to recruitment and conditions of service of persons appointed to public services and posts in connection with the affairs of the Union or any State.



  2. The Right to Information Act, 2005

    This act provides for the disclosure of information by public authorities and to ensure transparency and accountability in the administration of public services.



  3. The Central Vigilance Commission Act, 2003

    This act provides for the constitution of a Central Vigilance Commission to inquire into offenses committed by public servants and to promote integrity, transparency, and accountability in public administration.



  4. The Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988

    This act provides for the prevention of corruption and to establish institutions for the investigation and prosecution of offenses under the act.



  5. The Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850

    This act provides for the inquiry into the conduct of public servants and to establish procedures for their suspension, dismissal, or removal.



  6. The Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1954

    This act provides for the recruitment, training, and allocation of personnel for the Indian Administrative Service, which is responsible for the administration and implementation of policies and programs of the government.



  7. The All India Services Act, 1951

    This act provides for the establishment and regulation of All India Services, which include the Indian Administrative Service, Indian Police Service, and Indian Forest Service, to ensure the efficient and impartial administration of the Union and the States.





TAX LAW ACTS



Some of the major Tax law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. Income Tax Act, 1961

    This act provides for the levy, administration, collection, and recovery of income tax in India.



  2. Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017

    This act provides for the levy and collection of goods and services tax (GST) in India, which is a comprehensive indirect tax on the supply of goods and services.



  3. Central Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017

    This act provides for the levy of central goods and services tax (CGST) on the intra-state supply of goods and services in India.



  4. Integrated Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017

    This act provides for the levy of integrated goods and services tax (IGST) on the inter-state supply of goods and services in India.



  5. Customs Act, 1962

    This act provides for the levy, collection, and regulation of customs duties on the import and export of goods in India.



  6. Central Excise Act, 1944

    This act provides for the levy and collection of excise duty on the manufacture of goods in India.



  7. Service Tax Act, 1994

    This act provides for the levy and collection of service tax on certain taxable services in India.

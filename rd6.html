EMPLOYMENT LAW



Employment law is the area of law that governs the employer-employee relationship.


It covers a wide range of issues related to the workplace, such as hiring and firing practices, discrimination, employee benefits,
and hours, workplace safety, and employee privacy.


Employment law can be complex and may vary from one job or industry to another, but it's important for both employers and employees
to understand their rights and responsibilities under these laws.

TYPES:


There are several types of employment law that govern the relationship between employers and employees.
These laws may vary depending on the jurisdiction, but some common types of employment law include:



  1. Labor law: This type of law governs the relationship between employers and labor unions, and includes issues such as collective bargaining and strikes.

  2. Discrimination law: These laws prohibit discrimination in the workplace based on protected characteristics such as race, gender, religion, national origin, and disability.

  3. Family and medical leave law: These laws provide job-protected leave for eligible employees who need time off to care for a family member or for their own medical needs.

  4. Workplace safety law: These laws require employers to provide a safe working environment for their employees and establish safety standards for certain industries.

  5. Employee benefits law: These laws regulate employer-provided benefits such as health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.


OBJECTIVE:

The objective of employment law is to establish a fair and balanced relationship between employers and employees.

Employment law also seeks to promote equal employment opportunities for all individuals regardless of their race, gender, religion, national origin, or disability status.

The overall goal of employment law is to create a work environment that is free from discrimination, harassment, and unfair treatment.

It also aims to ensure that employers follow certain standards when it comes to hiring, firing, and managing their employees.

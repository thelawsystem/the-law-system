ADMINISTRATIVE LAW



Administrative law refers to the body of law that governs the activities of administrative agencies, such as government departments, commissions, boards, and bureaus.

These agencies have the power to make rules, regulations, and decisions that affect the rights and interests of individuals and organizations.

Administrative law sets out the rules and procedures that govern how administrative agencies operate and make decisions.

It also provides a framework for individuals and organizations to challenge decisions made by administrative agencies
if they believe those decisions are unfair or unlawful.

In addition, Administrative law is the set of rules and procedures that govern the actions of government agencies and
ensure that they operate in a fair and transparent manner.



TYPES:


There are several types of administrative law. Some of the most common types include:



  1. Rulemaking: This refers to the process by which administrative agencies make rules and regulations that have the force of law.

  2. Licensing and permitting: This refers to the process by which administrative agencies grant licenses and permits to individuals and organizations.

  3. Enforcement: This refers to the process by which administrative agencies enforce their rules and regulations.


Overall, administrative law is a broad field that encompasses many different types of legal processes and procedures.



OBJECTIVE:

The objective of administrative law is to ensure that government agencies operate in a fair, transparent, and accountable manner.

Administrative law aims to promote good governance by providing a framework for the exercise of administrative power.

Administrative law also seeks to protect individuals and organizations from arbitrary or unjust actions by government agencies.

Overall, the objective of administrative law is to promote the rule of law, protect individual rights and interests, and ensure that government agencies operate in a fair and transparent manner.

FAMILY LAW



Family law is an area of law that deals with legal issues related to family relationships, such as marriage,
divorce, child custody, and adoption.


Family law governs the legal rights and obligations of individuals within a family, as well as the legal processes involved in resolving family disputes.

Some common areas of family law include:


  1. Marriage and divorce: Family law governs the legal requirements for getting married and the legal process for getting divorced, including the division of property, spousal support, and child custody and support.

  2. Child custody and support: Family law governs the legal rights and obligations of parents in relation to their children, including child custody, visitation, and child support.

  3. Adoption: Family law governs the legal process for adopting a child, including the rights and responsibilities of adoptive parents.

  4. Domestic violence: Family law includes provisions for protecting victims of domestic violence, such as restraining orders and other legal remedies.

  5. Surrogacy and assisted reproduction: Family law governs the legal issues related to surrogacy, artificial insemination, and other forms of assisted reproduction.

  6. Paternity Law: This type of family law deals with the legal determination of paternity, including issues such as child support, custody, and visitation.

  7. Guardianship and Conservatorship Law: This type of family law governs the legal relationship between a guardian or conservator and a minor or incapacitated adult, including issues such as decision-making authority and financial management.


OBJECTIVE:

The objective of family law is to provide a legal framework that regulates the legal relationships and rights of individuals within a family unit.

The primary objectives of family law include:
Protecting the interests of children, Ensuring fair distribution of property, Providing legal protection for victims of domestic violence, Promoting stability and certainty.

Overall, the objective of family law is to promote the well-being of individuals within a family unit and to provide a legal framework for resolving family disputes and regulating family relationships.

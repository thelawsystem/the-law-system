Small Causes Court

The Small Causes Court, also known as the Small Claims Court, is a specialized court that handles cases related to small monetary disputes and minor civil matters.

Here are some general details about Small Causes Courts in India:



Jurisdiction:



Small Causes Courts typically have limited jurisdiction and handle cases involving small amounts of money or specific types of disputes.

The jurisdiction and monetary limits can vary between different states or union territories.



Structure:



Small Causes Courts are usually established as separate courts or divisions within the district court system.

They may have their own designated buildings or operate within the premises of the district court.



Case Types:



Small Causes Courts primarily deal with cases related to:



  • Small monetary claims and debts
  • Rental and tenancy disputes
  • Consumer complaints and disputes
  • Matters related to specific legislation (e.g., the Rent Control Act)

    • Procedure:



    The procedures and rules followed in Small Causes Courts are usually simpler and more informal compared to regular civil courts.
    They aim to provide an accessible and expeditious resolution to small disputes.



    Representation:



    In Small Causes Courts, parties may or may not require legal representation.

    The rules regarding legal representation can vary, and individuals may choose to represent themselves or engage an advocate based on the specific case and jurisdiction.

CRIMINAL LAW

Criminal law is the part of the law that deals with crimes. It is a set of rules that society uses to punish people who have done things that are considered very wrong, such as stealing, hurting others, or committing murder. Criminal law helps to keep people safe by punishing those who break the rules and making sure that everyone knows what is considered unacceptable behavior.



There are several types

1.Substantive Criminal Law
2.Procedural Criminal Law

Substantive Criminal Law

In this type of criminal law defines what conduct is considered a crime and specifies the punishments for such conduct. It includes a wide range of offenses, from minor misdemeanors like traffic violations to serious felonies like murder, rape, or robbery.
The elements typically include:

  1. Actus reus: The criminal act itself, such as stealing or killing.

  2. Mens rea: The mental state of the defendant, such as intent, knowledge, recklessness, or negligence.

  3. Concurrence: The requirement that the actus reus and mens rea occurred at the same time.

  4. Causation: The requirement that the defendant's conduct caused the harm or injury in question.

Substantive criminal law defines what behavior is considered a crime and specifies the punishments for such conduct. It sets out the elements of a crime, such as the act itself and the mental state of the defendant. The range of punishments can include fines, imprisonment, probation, or even the death penalty.



Procedural Criminal Law

This type of criminal law sets out the rules and procedures for handling criminal cases, including the steps that must be followed by law enforcement officials, prosecutors, defense attorneys, judges, and juries. It covers everything from the investigation and arrest of suspects to the trial, sentencing, and appeal processes.


Procedural law covers a wide range of legal matters, including civil and criminal cases, administrative law, and international law. It ensures that legal proceedings are conducted fairly and efficiently, and that all parties are given an opportunity to present their case.



Major criminal law acts in India along with the year they were enacted:



  1. Indian Penal Code (IPC) - 1860

    The IPC is the primary criminal law act in India.
    It was enacted in 1860 and defines various criminal offenses and their punishments.



  2. Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) - 1973

    The CrPC lays down the procedure for the investigation, trial, and punishment of criminal offenses.



  3. Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act (NDPS Act) - 1985

    This act regulates the production, sale, and consumption of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances in India.



  4. Prevention of Corruption Act (PCA) - 1988

    The PCA aims to prevent corruption and punish public officials who engage in corrupt practices.



  5. Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act (POCSO Act) - 2012

    This act provides protection to children from sexual offenses and lays down stringent punishment for offenders.



  6. Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act - 2000

    This act deals with juvenile offenders and provides for their care, protection, and rehabilitation.



  7. Indian Evidence Act - 1872

    This act lays down the rules of evidence in Indian courts.



  8. Dowry Prohibition Act - 1961

    This act prohibits the giving or taking of dowry in marriages.



  9. Information Technology Act - 2000

    This act deals with cyber crimes and provides for their punishment.



  10. The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act- 2013

    This act provides for the prevention, prohibition, and redressal of sexual harassment of women at the workplace.



OBJECTIVE:

The objective of criminal law is to establish a system of laws that defines criminal offenses and outlines the punishment that can be imposed on individuals who violate those laws.

The punishments may include fines, imprisonment, probation, community service, and in some cases, the death penalty.
